My research develops a structural approach to observability, metric structure, temporal ordering, and constructional form. These are treated as consequences of projection, finite admissibility, and relational invariance — rather than as primitive elements of a physical theory.

The work is organized into three interconnected lines: (1) structural foundations of physics beyond the Standard Model — projection, finite-rank observability, and the emergence of time; (2) boundary observability, wave projection, and interface-induced dimensional reduction; (3) ancient Egyptian metrology, structural invariants, and constructional non-invertibility.

LINE 01

Structural foundations of physics beyond the Standard Model: projection, finite-rank observability, and the emergence of time

A structural framework that precedes the Standard Model and any specific dynamical theory. Projection — rather than spacetime, fields, or particles — is taken as the primary constraint. Finite-rank observability, operational metric structure, gauge redundancy, and temporal ordering emerge from non-invertible representational access and Noetherian conservation requirements, independently of any particular physical realization.

Projection-Induced Singular Value Decomposition of Irrational Scale Invariant
Shows that linear discretization of an irrational scale relation defines a non-isometric projection operator admitting a natural SVD. Finite spectral resolution enforces truncation, yielding finite effective rank. Non-closure, dispersion, and discrete holonomy arise as unavoidable residuals. The analysis is operator-theoretic and model-independent.

Boundary Metric from Projection-Induced Finite Rank
Analyzes how a notion of metric necessarily arises when observables are obtained through a finite-rank, non-isometric projection. The framework is pre-geometric and pre-dynamical: the metric describes limits of resolvable structure imposed by projection, not spatial distance.

LINE 02

Boundary observability, wave projection, and interface-induced dimensional reduction

Structural limits of phase observability in wave systems under boundary constraints. Interface-induced dimensional reduction, non-invertible bulk-to-boundary maps, and guided or phase-locked boundary responses in sensing configurations.

Dimensional Reduction as a Limit to Boundary Phase Inference
When admissibility and boundary compatibility suppress normal propagating transport, an exact boundary-induced dimensional reduction occurs. The bulk-to-boundary phase mapping is structurally non-invertible: boundary phase measurements cannot uniquely reconstruct the underlying bulk elastodynamic state. This limitation is geometric, not experimental.

Boundary-Induced Phase Locking and Guided Helical Modes in SAR Doppler Tomography of the Giza Plateau
Analysis of phase-based SAR Doppler tomographic products within a framework of structural non-invertibility and boundary-limited observability. Using public polarimetric datasets from the Giza Plateau, results demonstrate highly constrained global phase organization with sustained longitudinal coherence and symmetry-selective phase locking. LINE 03

Ancient Egyptian metrology, structural invariants, and constructional non-invertibility

Historical metrology and structural invariance in ancient construction processes. The Egyptian cubit system as a projection of non-linear invariants into administratively usable linear ratios, approached via structural analysis rather than anthropometric reconstruction.

The Short Cubit as a Structural Invariant: Dilatation, Discrete Generation, and Metrological Projection in Ancient Egypt
The Short Cubit is reframed at two pre-metrical levels: as a dilatation normalization expressed by the adimensional factor 1/√5, and as a discrete generative step represented by the non-tessellating angle π/7. The ratio Cᵣ/Cₛ = φ²/√5 is derived as a normalized golden curvature. Explains why no unique Short Cubit length should be expected.

Metrological Non-Invertibility and Projection Constants in Historical Scale-Based Construction
Argues that the Short Cubit ratio and the seked are not empirical coincidences but structural residues of a non-invertible projection from scale dynamics to observable construction. A projection constant is identified: a dimensionless scalar that survives realization while the underlying scale action is irreversibly discarded. Linear and angular measures organize into a coupled metric–angular lattice within a restricted observable domain.
