Gauge grammar · Q8 · 2026

Observable Gauge Algebra from Finite-Rank Majorana Projection

The observable gauge algebra of the Standard Model is derived from a single structural input: the rank-5 non-invertible projection of a Majorana bulk spinor onto a four-dimensional boundary. No gauge group, coupling constants, or symmetry-breaking mechanism are postulated. The Majorana condition forces five admissible spinorial roles at the boundary. One is a confined mode that cannot enter independently: it must be lifted through the minimal spinorial cover, a necessity of spinorial transport. This lift generates a primitive spinorial generator whose norm decomposes into a redistributable sector and an irreducible residue — the carrier of the unbroken electromagnetic symmetry. The redistributable sector re-enters as observable gauge modes at an adjoint closure cost fixed by the ratio of the adjoint dimension to the spinorial doublet dimension. This cost is the mass of the charged bosons. Their ratio to the total dynamic sector is the electroweak mixing angle, an unconditional theorem. The lock-in holonomy identifies the boundary algebra as TL4(φ). Gauge physics is the boundary grammar of projected Majorana spinorial transport.
