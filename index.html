The central claim of this research is that finite-rank non-isometric projection is the primary generative constraint of physical structure. When a domain of observability is obtained through such a projection, metric structure, temporal ordering, gauge redundancy, and observable form are not primitive inputs — they are necessary residuals of irreversible information loss under projection.

This is a single research programme developed at three scales. LINE 01 derives the principle in its most general, pre-physical form: starting from a conserved Noetherian flow under restricted accessibility, metric, time, and gauge emerge as the only structures compatible with non-invertibility. LINE 02 identifies what the same principle implies when projection meets a physical boundary: bulk-to-boundary maps become structurally non-injective, and dimensional reduction is exact rather than approximate. LINE 03 shows that the principle was already structurally operative in historical construction: the Short Cubit ratio and the seked are projection constants that survived metrological realization while the underlying scale dynamics was irreversibly discarded — the same mechanism, at a different scale of instantiation.
